Pracs 6-9: Group Project Work

In these later practicals, you will work in your project teams to develop the project assignment.
Each of these practicals will start with a standup meeting for each group.
Here is a short video linked in the subject outline about stand-ups to help everyone understand them.

Make sure you actually stand for your meeting! This IS what many industry teams do every single day, so this is preparing you for industry.
These meetings are NOT for you to present to the lecturer – it is for you to discuss your work; and the lecturer will just go around and witness the meetings.

Here’s how each standup should go. Each person tells the rest of their team:

  • What they worked on last week (since the last meeting)
  • Any road-blocks or things they’re stuck on (and might need help with)
  • What they plan to achieve in the coming week (before the next meeting)

After your short (no more than 5 minutes) meeting, get to work, developing your project site, including working out your development and deployment workflow.

Vagrant

Vagrant is a useful tool for configuring and running Virtual Environments on your local computer. You will need VirtualBox

Here are some things to get started with (install): VVV for WordPress: https://github.com/Varying-Vagrant-Vagrants/VVV Add-on Dashboard: https://github.com/topdown/VVV-Dashboard Site Wizard: https://github.com/bradp/vv

WordMove: a handy tool for copying (deploying) from your Vagrant localhost environment to another (staging/production) site: https://github.com/welaika/wordmove You install this IN Vagrant - after vagrant ssh

Mac GUI for managing Vagrant boxes: http://vagrantmanager.com

And a popular Vagrant box for a more generic Web server (LAMP stack; no CMS installed): https://github.com/scotch-io/scotch-box
