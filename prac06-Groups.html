Practical - Group Meeting

Aim:

  • To get to know the other members of your project team
  • To get setup with project management tools (Trello, GitHub, Slack) for effective collaboration
  • To begin discussing your ideas for the project process and website

Go:

(Project and other items are on LearnJCU under Assessment.)

  1. Get together with your team - sit in something like a circle; you don't each need to use a computer
  2. Get to know each other:

    Play this fun game...
    Each person take a turn: tell your team your name and 3 things about yourself, one of which is not true...
    Everyone else in the team has to guess the lie :)
    You've learned a few things about each of your teammates - nice.
    Working in a team is better if you know the people you're working with.

  3. Note: If any of your group members are missing from this session, you can email them using LearnJCU. Select Tools > Send Email > Select Users, then email them and let them know you missed them and when your next group meeting is. You could also trying sending a message in Slack; everyone's username should be like @firstnamelastname.
  4. Follow these instructions about setting up the project management tools
  5. Discuss and store your decisions for your group agreement on the card for this in your Trello board.

    Answering these questions will help your team discuss your plan for working together for the collaborative process.
    Some questions require you to think carefully, discuss options and make a plan.
    Complete these questions on your card in Trello:
    • List the names, contact detalis and GitHub usernames of each member of your team.
      Note here anyone who was away for this first prac/meeting.
    • What preferred methods of communication has your team decided on?
      (You must have a plan for communicating regularly and effectively.)
    • What are your meeting arrangements?
      (When and where are you going to meet outside prac time? weekly, twice a week... in the labs, at a team member's home, etc.)
    • What should a team member do if a planned meeting can’t be attended due to unforeseen circumstances?
    • What arrangements have you agreed on for what to do if a team member is not working effectively? 
      (E.g. if a member does not attend meetings, does not respond to communication attempts or does not complete assigned work.)
    • What can each team member contribute to the project?
      (List each team member's name with their currently anticipated role within the team.)
    • What challenges do you see for this collaboration?
  6. When you have completed all of the above, show your tutor (your group agreement in Trello, your GitHub repo with collaborators, your integrated Slack group)... then:
    Begin discussing your ideas for the project, taking notes in your Trello board (use the "Ideas" list).
    Quickly revise the project details if you need to.

 

From now on...

We are now in the second phase of the subject - the teamwork project. Each week in the practical, your tutor will meet with your team to evaluate your progress and provide feedback on your website and teamwork.

Your tutor will take notes on:

  • attendance (including who is missing)
  • progress
  • GitHub use - you will show your GitHub commits and the tutor can see who is doing any work. Make sure you are involved and committing
  • any issues or problems you have
